Why Phoenix Pride Project Exists

Our Story

I am Ronnel Jones, and I am building Phoenix Pride Project because I have seen what happens when young adults are rejected for who they are. I have watched the gaps between “services that exist” and “services that actually help” swallow whole futures.

Las Vegas is a national hotspot for trafficking. Hundreds of young adults are identified as victims here every year. LGBTQ+ young adults face unique risks due to family rejection, homelessness, and discrimination. These factors increase susceptibility to exploitation and trafficking. And too many of the shelters, clinics, and programs meant to help are not safe for queer and trans young adults.

I am not a clinician. I am not a politician. I am a person who believes that connection is the first form of survival. Phoenix Pride Project starts there.

Three P’s. One Purpose.

Phoenix — because we believe in rising. Not once, but as many times as it takes.

Pride — because identity is not the problem. It is the power.

Project — because this is work. Ongoing, imperfect, collaborative work.

3P is not just a nickname. It is a reminder that every part of this name matters.

What We Stand For

1. Empowerment

We believe in the strength and resilience of LGBTQ+ young adult survivors. Our programs foster self-advocacy, leadership, and personal growth.

2. Inclusivity & Affirmation

We create safe, welcoming spaces for all, regardless of sexual orientation, gender identity, race, or background. Every person is seen, respected, and valued.

3. Survivor-Centered Care

The voices, experiences, and needs of survivors guide every aspect of our work. We honor autonomy and dignity above all.

4. Community & Collaboration

We partner with survivors, peer mentors, local nonprofits, and advocates to strengthen networks and expand access to resources.

5. Equity & Justice

We address systemic barriers and discrimination that disproportionately impact LGBTQ+ young adults. Our advocacy challenges root causes.

6. Integrity & Accountability

We operate with transparency and ethical stewardship. We hold ourselves accountable to the communities we serve.

7. Healing & Hope

We believe healing is possible. Through trauma-informed care and unwavering support, we build pathways to recovery and a brighter future.
