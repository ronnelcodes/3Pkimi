What We Are Working Toward

These programs represent the full vision of Phoenix Pride Project. We are not running all of them today. We are starting with the Phoenix Navigation Network and building outward. Everything listed here is grounded in trauma-informed, LGBTQ+ affirming practice.

1. Healing & Wellness

  • Phoenix Rising Counseling Program — Trauma-informed therapy, art therapy, and peer support circles.
  • Healing Through Identity — LGBTQ+ affirming mental health services and gender-affirming care navigation.
  • Wellness Wednesdays — Weekly drop-in sessions for yoga, mindfulness, nutrition, and self-care education.

2. Housing & Safety

  • Safe Nest Initiative — Emergency housing for survivors with wraparound services.
  • Transitional Thrive Housing — Six to eighteen month housing with case management and life skills support.
  • Safety & Advocacy Hub — Legal advocacy, safety planning, and navigation of law enforcement and immigration systems.

3. Education & Employment

  • EmpowerEd — GED prep, tutoring, and college application support.
  • PrideWorks — Job readiness training, resume building, and workplace rights education.
  • Career Launchpad — Paid internships, mentorships, and partnerships with LGBTQ+ affirming employers.

Note: Scholarships are planned for Year 2, pending 501(c)(3) status and infrastructure.

4. Leadership & Community Building

  • Survivor Leadership Academy — Public speaking, advocacy training, and leadership development.
  • Peer Navigator Program — Trained survivor mentors guiding participants through service systems.
  • Community Pride Circles — Monthly gatherings for connection, healing, and identity celebration.

5. Outreach & Prevention

  • Street Outreach Team — Harm reduction, resource distribution, and crisis intervention.
  • Know Your Rights Workshops — Legal literacy for LGBTQ+ young adults and survivors.
  • Youth Prevention Ambassadors — Peer-led education in schools and shelters for trafficking prevention and safety.

What Is Active Now: The Phoenix Navigation Network

This is our starting point. No building. No full-time staff. Just trained volunteers and real connections.

Peer Navigator Program

Five volunteer navigators trained in trauma-informed care, active listening, and resource knowledge. Background checks required. Monthly supervision with the Executive Director.

Know Your Rights Workshops (Quarterly)

Virtual format, co-hosted with local legal partners. Topics: housing discrimination, police interaction, foster care rights, workplace protections. Recorded for later access.

Digital Resource Hub

A curated referral directory on our website. Simple intake form. Categorized resources: housing, legal, health, food, crisis. Mobile-friendly.

Community Pride Circles (Monthly)

Donor-facing storytelling events. Survivor speakers with paid honoraria and full consent protocols. Suggested donation $25–$100. Builds our monthly donor base.

Service Pathway

  1. Intake — Phone, web, or partner referral
  2. Needs Assessment — Non-clinical, strengths-based
  3. Navigation Plan — Prioritized needs, resource matching
  4. Warm Referral — 3P contacts the receiving organization on the client’s behalf
  5. Follow-Up — Within 48 hours, ongoing as needed
  6. Outcome Tracking — Housing secured, legal aid connected, etc.
