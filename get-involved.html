Be a Peer Navigator

We are recruiting our first five volunteer peer navigators. This is not a casual volunteer role. It requires a 4-hour trauma-informed training, a background check ($50–$75, covered by us), and a commitment to monthly supervision.

What you will do:

  • Connect LGBTQ+ young adult survivors and those escaping survival sex work to housing, legal, health, and crisis resources
  • Maintain boundaries and confidentiality
  • Document interactions for outcome tracking
  • Attend monthly check-ins with the Executive Director

Who we are looking for:

  • Survivors, allies, social work students, retired professionals
  • People who can listen without fixing
  • People who understand that trust is built slowly

Organizations, Let’s Talk

We do not duplicate services. We connect young adults to them. If your organization serves LGBTQ+ young adults, trafficking survivors, or those escaping survival sex work in Clark County, we want to know you.

We are especially interested in partnerships with:

  • Housing providers and shelters
  • Legal aid organizations
  • Mental health clinics with LGBTQ+ affirming care
  • Schools and workforce programs
  • Other LGBTQ+ service organizations
